\section{Optical depth}

The equation for optical depth for vertical incidence:
\begin{equation}
\tau(z,\lambda, 0) = \sum_{j} \sigma_{j}^{a}(\lambda) \int_{z}^{\infty} n_j(z') \,dz'
\end{equation}
With adding the geometry for non-vertical incidence this is for some angles up to about 60\si{\degree}:
\begin{equation}
\tau(z,\lambda, \chi) =\frac{1}{cos \chi} \sum_{j} \sigma_{j}^{a}(\lambda) \int_{z}^{\infty} n_j(z') \,dz'
\end{equation}
This gets more complicated for even higher incidence angles up to 90\si{\degree}. 
\begin{equation}
\tau(z,\lambda, \chi) = \sum_{j} \sigma_{j}^{a}(\lambda) \int_{z}^{\infty} n_j(z') \left[1 -  \left( \frac{R_E + z_0}{R_E + z'} \right)^2 sin^2 \chi_0 \right]^{-1/2}  \,dz'
\end{equation}
For my calculations I used equation (2), as this reduces to equation(1) for vertical incidence anyways as cos(0)=1. 

But first the cross sections had to be interpolated to the wavelength resolution in the irradiance file. I used np.interpolate as this gave the best fiting results. But I get 0 for all wavelength after 100nm as there is no more data in the cross section file.

EUV fluxes for different heights and wavelengths can be calculated as:
\begin{equation}
I(z,\lambda) = I^{\infty}(\lambda)exp(-\tau(z,\lambda,\chi))
\end{equation}
$I^{\infty}(\lambda)$ is the photon flux at infinity, or at the top of the thermosphere.
